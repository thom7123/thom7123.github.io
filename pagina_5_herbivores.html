Herbivores

Giant Herbivores

Dinosaur Dino Info
The Brachiosaurus is probably the most famous long neck dinosaur of the Jurassic franchise. The Brachiosaurus is the first dinosaur we see in the movies and is one of the most iconic scenes in the series. There is a special feeder for giant herbivores: the tall feeder, it is a long stick with leafs on it, like a tree. The Brachiosaurus needs, compared to other dinosaurs, a lot of space to live in. When you're building the enclosure it feels like 5 times the size of a regular enclosure. The Brachiosaurus is found in North America on the expedition map of the expedition center.
Another large herbivore is the Diplodocus. The diplodocus has never physically appeared in movies, only as a toy in Jurassic Park and as a painting in Jurassic World. The Diplodocus needs, compared to the Brachiosaurus, a lot less space to live in and is a lot faster satisfied. The Diplodocus is found in North America on the expedition map of the expedition center.

Medium Herbivores

Dinosaur Dino Info
The Edmontosaurus is one of the first dinosaurs you get on the island and is necessary for the security division mission on Isla Matanceros. This dinosaur can be in large groups but needs only a few in one enclosure to please it. This dinosaur is very easily pleased and doesn't need to much space. The Edmontosaurus appeared in The Lost World: Jurassic Park as a skull and in Jurassic world Fallen Kingdom as a skeleton. The Edmontosaurus is found in North America on the expedition map of the expedition center.

Armored Herbivores

Dinosaur Dino Info
The Triceratops is probably the most famous of the armored dinosaurs. The Triceratops has its name because of the three spikes on its head. The Triceratops needs at least one other Triceratops in the same enclosure as him because it is a social createre (like most of the herbivores). The Triceratops has appeared in: Jurassic Park, The Lost World: Jurassic Park, Jurassic Park III, Jurassic World, Jurassic World Fallen Kingdom and Jurassic world Dominion (in other words every JP/JW movie). The Triceratops is found in North America on the expedition map of the expedition center.
The Stegosaurus is also a very famous dinosaur with its large spikes on its tail and the plates on its back. The Stegosaurus has appeared in the movies: The Lost World: Jurassic Park, Jurassic Park III, Jurassic world, Jurassic World Fallen Kingdom and Jurassic World Dominion (it was only an Embyo in Jurassic Park and not a full grown dinosaur). The Stegosaurus is even a very social dinosaur with a group of at least 5 stegosauruses in one enclosure. The Stegosaurus is found in North America and Europe on the expedition map of the expedition center.
